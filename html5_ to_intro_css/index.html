2022: The rapid growth of A.I.

In 2015 an organization named OpenAi became determined to push the boundaries of artificial intelligence. By 2022 Chatgpt was introduced to the public and instantly started producing immense conversation on social media platforms. Though there have been A.I. on smaller scales in the past, Chatgpt sets itself apart with 175 billion parameters which enables it to generate text that is far more coherent, contextually relevant, and human-like than previous models. Since then, more and more A.I. tools come out. Such ass an app that allows you to enter a prompt, and within seconds have a full work of art. No less than a year later people began using A.I. to create songs with established artist voices. Making it seem as though You're favorite artist was dropping new music but if you really listen it is distinguishable. Fast forward to today and theres A.I. animators, almost every company uses an A.I. chat box. Apple has even announced their next phone will be focused on its A.I. and its capabilities. WIth such a rapid increase of A.I. in the last few years, it's scary to think about what's to come even 10 years down the line.

My Teacher's a Robot!

A big question is how will this effect our school system? It certainly has its advantages as well as its disadvantages. In a positive perspective, these new A.I. tools will help innovate our learning system as we can quickly gather a vast amount of date in just a few seconds. Of course the possibilities are endless but here's just a few ways bringing A.I could be beneficial;

  • Quicker way of gathering information
  • Great rough drafts for essays or projects
  • Capable of taking notes an individual may have missed
On the contrary it could also stump the learning process as most people attempt to use the entire A.I. system as their work and have it write out their work rather than just using it as a tool. Luckily administrators caught on and there is now A.I. with the capability to detect if work was done by A.I. It's unlikely to see A.I. used in a school system but it ponders the thought, how long before our classes are taught by A.I. models?
